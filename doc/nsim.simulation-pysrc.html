 27  """simulation framework for extracellular recordings 
 28   
 29  This module provides a discrete time event simulation framework for objects 
 30  placed in a 3-dimensional space (refered to as the scene). Objects have a 
 31  distinct position in the scene for each point in time. Time is descretized to 
 32  samples with a fixed sample rate. This way the realtime/observed simulation 
 33  speed is decoupled from the internal time scale and can be changed on the fly. 
 34   
 35  Objets are classified as either Recorders or Neurons and are modeled as 
 36  subclasses of SimObject. A SimObject is a set of scene coordinates, along with a 
 37  set of applicable behaviors. E.g. Neurons can have a firing statistic. 
 38   
 39  The simulation is conducted in frames. A Frame is a batch of samples that is 
 40  simulated as one step of the simulation. Any input to the simulation while a 
 41  frame is processed is queued and applied at the end of the frame (applies to 
 42  movement etc.). 
 43   
 44  The framework is tailored towards simulation of extracellular recordings with 
 45  several (1 to 10) Recorders and clusters of Neurons (3 clusters, 10 each). 
 46   
 47  Recorded data can be either saved to files (HDF5) or broadcasted to the net via 
 48  TCP/IP with a simple protocoll. 
 49   
 50  Definition of terms: 
 51  :SCENE: 
 52      The SCENE is the spatial context of the simulation (usually a 3D space with 
 53      euclidean coordinates). Time is advanced with respect to a fixed sampling 
 54      rate and the SCENE has a destinct configuration per sample. There is only 
 55      one SCENE. 
 56  :OBJECT: 
 57      An OBJECT is a subclass of SimObject. OBJECTs model all agents and sources 
 58      in the SCENE, they are administrated as items of the BaseSimulation instance 
 59      . 
 60  :SAMPLE: 
 61      A SAMPLE is the smallest discrete time step known to the simulation. It is 
 62      thus the unit of measurement for the temporal sampling. A SAMPLE i related 
 63      to real time via the SAMPLE_RATE, denoting the SAMPLEs in a second (Hz). 
 64  :FRAME: 
 65      A FRAME is a fixed time period of n samples. The simulation is advanced in 
 66      frames. It is computationally attractive to simulate FRAMEs as oposed to 
 67      single SAMPLEs, as most numerical algorithms perform better on batch data 
 68      than on single SAMPLEs. Also timer implementations of normal systems may not 
 69      provide the resolution neccessary to simulate common sampling rates (like 
 70      16kHz or 32kHz) in real time. 
 71  """
